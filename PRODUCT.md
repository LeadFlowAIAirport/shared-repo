# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local, year-round service-business owners — **plumbing and electrical companies first**, with
the same offer applying to other trades (HVAC, roofing, cleaning, landscaping, painting, junk
removal, pressure washing). The reader is a busy, non-technical trades owner: often on a phone,
between jobs, skeptical of "marketing companies." They have ~10 seconds to decide whether this
is worth their time. They speak in jobs booked and calls missed, not funnels and KPIs.

## Product Purpose

LeadFlow (placeholder brand name) is an **early-stage** AI automation agency that helps local
service businesses capture more leads, respond faster, and book more jobs. The offer is three
systems:

1. **Lead Generation System** — ads/funnels → high-converting landing page or questionnaire →
   qualified leads pushed toward booking.
2. **AI Receptionist / Appointment-Setting System** — handles missed calls, texts, and form
   submissions; responds fast, answers basics, captures job details, books appointments.
3. **Local Visibility System** — improves local search presence, reputation, and findability.

Success = a trades owner understands the problem and the offer clearly, trusts the operation
enough to book an intro call, and does so without being promised any specific outcome.

## Brand Personality

Plainspoken, direct, dependable, competent. Talks like a capable tradesperson, not a marketer:
short sentences, concrete nouns, no jargon. Confident without hype. The voice of someone who
shows up, does the work, and explains it straight.

3-word personality: **grounded · direct · dependable**.

Emotional goal: a skeptical owner feels *understood* and *reassured* — "these people actually
get my business, and they're not going to oversell me."

## Anti-references

- "Guru agency" energy: countdown timers, "book NOW", hustle-bro hype.
- Generic SaaS / AI-agency template: electric-blue accent, Inter, endless identical icon-cards,
  gradient-on-white hero, a wall of fake logos.
- Fake social proof of any kind: testimonials, reviews, star ratings, "trusted by 100+",
  invented stats, result guarantees. **This agency has no clients yet and says so by omission —
  credibility comes from clarity, not claims.**
- Cold, abstract Big-Tech minimalism that talks over the reader's head.

## Design Principles

- **Clarity is the credibility.** With no track record to lean on, the plain explanation of the
  problem and the offer IS the trust-builder. Say exactly what each system does.
- **Speak the owner's language.** Concrete trade scenarios (the 11 PM emergency call, the quote
  that never got followed up) over marketing abstractions.
- **Show the mechanism, don't claim the result.** Demonstrate *how* missed-call recovery works;
  never assert numbers or outcomes we can't stand behind.
- **Built like good tools.** Sturdy, honest, high-contrast, nothing decorative that doesn't earn
  its place. The design should feel as dependable as the service.
- **Honest by construction.** No element, image, or number may imply clients or results we don't
  have.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast. Full keyboard navigation and visible focus states.
All motion has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative and never gates content visibility.
Large, tap-friendly targets (mobile-first — the audience reads on phones in the field).
